    About me

    My PhD programme of study is the EPSRC-funded Centre for Doctoral Training in Agri-Food Robotics and Autonomous Systems (AgriFoRwArdS, grant reference: EP/ S023917/1). The AgriFoRwArdS research community focuses on developing and integrating robotics and autonomous systems into agriculture and food manufacturing.

    As part of the AgriFoRwArdS programme, I completed an MSc in Robotics and Autonomous Systems at the University of Lincoln in 2022. Prior to this, I graduated from the University of Cambridge with a first-class integrated MEng in Manufacturing Engineering.

    I am passionate about transforming the food system into one that is healthy for both people and the planet. Technologies, such as robotics, may play a role in this transformation. However, implementing new technologies can only be sustainable if such technology management decisions consider the needs and perspectives of multiple stakeholders. My research will explore how these different stakeholder perspectives, such as the environmental and social perspectives, can be integrated into technology selection decisions.
